⚠️ Важные предупреждения

How accurate is a bolus insulin calculator? +
A bolus calculator applies your personal ICR and ISF values to a standard formula used by endocrinologists worldwide. The mathematical result is only as accurate as the values you enter. The formula does not account for: active insulin on board (IOB) from previous injections, the glycemic index of food, physical activity (which increases insulin sensitivity), illness or infection (which increases insulin resistance), stress hormones, alcohol consumption, or injection site absorption differences. Always treat the result as a starting point for discussion with your diabetes care team, not as a final dose.
What is the difference between mmol/L and mg/dL? +
Both are units used to measure blood glucose concentration. mmol/L (millimoles per liter) is used in most of Europe, Canada, Australia, and Asia. mg/dL (milligrams per deciliter) is used in the United States and some other countries. To convert: multiply mmol/L by 18.016 to get mg/dL. For example, 5.5 mmol/L = 99 mg/dL. This calculator supports both units — use the toggle at the top of the calculator screen to switch.
Can I use this calculator for Type 2 diabetes? +
This calculator uses the standard ICR/ISF bolus formula, designed for people on a basal-bolus insulin regimen — most commonly people with Type 1 diabetes and some with insulin-dependent Type 2 diabetes. If your doctor has prescribed bolus insulin and provided ICR and ISF values, this calculator may be useful. Always consult your diabetes care team before changing your insulin dosing approach.
